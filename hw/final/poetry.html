poetry

note to self

be soft.

i whisper in my own ear,

be still.

let your words be paced and velvet, like the notes on a piano.



be soft.

softness is not synonymous with weakness;

louder is not synonymous with stronger.

be quiet. feel.



be soft.

coarse is still beautiful,

you are still beautiful.

give direction to your fire.

focus on the amber glow it emits,

not the harsh burn of its flames.



be soft.

immerse yourself in feeling, in vulnerability,

wrap yourself in silk robes of pure gold.

exist.

how to be racially ambiguous

be prepared to feel alone.

instead of poems woven from adenine, cyosine, guanine, and thymine,

your DNA will form a single word:

enigmatic.



be prepared to hear lots of questions and assertions.

lots.

they will ask why you look exotic, and what exactly are you,

and there will be a kid in your third-grade class who will obnoxiously shout

that it says ‘black’ on your state assessment packet and there must be some mistake.

the other kids will laugh.



you’ll feel insecure that you don’t look like your family members,

and some days the concerned looks from the families at Denny’s when you go out with your cousins

will get to you.

you’ll spend hours in your room crying because you feel like you don’t belong.



make sure your physical attributes are in excess:

your hair must be too coarse to be white,

but your skin must be too fair to be black,

and if you really want to throw them for a loop

make sure your eyes are green.



I know that it isn’t easy,

but try not to think about the girl at your graduation party who said

‘so wait... this is what your family looks like?’

she was just curious.

they always are.
